Why Make Pasta?

Pasta is amazing - we all know that. Making pasta from scratch is the only way to make it.
The italian invention has been around for years, yet pasta still remains one of the most beloved foods in the world.
You get the authentic feel of making it yourself, and then understanding the value of tasty pasta by having full control over your own ingredients.

Ingredients

1 cup all-purpose flour
½ teaspoon salt
1 egg, beaten
2 tablespoons water (Optional)
2 tablespoons olive oil
1 medium onion, finely chopped
1 teaspoon packed brown sugar
2 garlic cloves, minced
1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
2 teaspoons dried basil
1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
1 bay leaf
3/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon pepper

Steps for Pasta

1. First, make a nest with the flour on a clean work surface.
2. Next, use your hands to gently mix in the flour.
3. Then, knead!
4. When the dough comes together, shape it into a ball and wrap it in plastic wrap
5. After the dough rests, slice it into 4 pieces.
6. Use a rolling pin or your hands to gently flatten one into an oval disk.
7. Then, run it through the widest setting of your pasta maker.
8. Next, fold the dough… if you want to.
9. Then, fold it in half lengthwise to form a rectangle.
10 .Once you’ve folded the dough, roll it out to your desired thickness.
11. Repeat these steps with the remaining dough pieces.
12. Finally, cut and cook the pasta!

Steps for Sauce

1. Heat the olive oil over high heat until it shimmers.
2. Saute the onions, stirring frequently, until they soften and shine, about three minutes.
3. The onions should sizzle and hiss as they cook.
4. Add the garlic. Stir to combine(This prevents the garlic from burning!).
5. Cook an additional two minutes.
6. Add the basil, red pepper flakes, salt, and sugar.
7. Stir to combine, and Add the butter.
8. Stir, cook for about a minute.
9. Add 1/2 can of the crushed tomatoes. Scrape the bottom of the pan to remove any stuck on bits.
10 Reduce heat to low. Add remaining tomatoes. Stir in 1/4 cup water.
If the sauce seems too thick, add additional water.
11. Allow sauce to simmer for 10 minutes to up to one hour.
12. Finally, add the sauce to your freshly made pasta!
